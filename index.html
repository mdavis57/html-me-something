Pets Throughout the Years

Over the years I've gotten the privilege experience the companionship of caring for pets. Whether they're fuzzy or scaly, I have a soft spot in my heart for all of them.



Max

Max is a Golden Retreiver Chow mix. He was my first childhood pet; we got him as a puppy when I was justa baby. We truly got to grow up together.



Tiki and Rex

I had two Leopard Geckos for over 10 years. They were fun pets to have and it was fun feeding them crickets and meal worms.



Oreo and Vanilla

My brother and I each had two mice growing up as well. They are cute and sweet, but I don't recommend them as pets. They don't live a long life, they poop alot, and some can be very mean.



Maddie

Maddie was my hamster. Hamsters make excellent pets, but are escape artists. One time Maddie escaped for 3 days. When she was ready to be found, she just strolled into the living room while we were watching a movie.



Little Butter

Little Butter was my black cat. She was sweet and playful. She was named after the WiFi code that came with our apartment



Amber

Amber is my current dog, a sweet pitbull mutt. We adopted her at three years old and she is now twelve. She still acts like a puppy.



Adopt

When you are considering getting a new animal companion, please consider adoption. The St Louis Humane Society is one option that has many branches and offer many aanimals ready for a forever home. There are many species, ages, personalities, and breeds to choose from. You will feel good knowing you rescued your pet and saved their life. They will be forever grateful. Adopt, don't shop!
